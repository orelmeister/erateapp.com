Common Reasons E-Rate Applications Get Denied

Many denials result from technicalities and procedural errors—not ineligibility. Here are the most common issues we successfully appeal:

Form 470 Procedural Errors

Missing or incorrect information on the competitive bidding form, insufficient bid evaluation documentation.

Form 471 Application Issues

Incorrect service start dates, mismatched FRNs, or Category 1/Category 2 classification errors.

PIA Review Response Failures

Incomplete PIA responses, missed deadlines, or documentation that didn't satisfy reviewer questions.

Competitive Bidding Violations

Alleged bid manipulation, insufficient wait periods, or improper vendor selection criteria.

Invoice Deadline Issues

Late invoice submissions, incorrect BEAR/SPI forms, or payment disputes with vendors.

Eligibility Determinations

Incorrect discount rate calculations, entity eligibility questions, or service eligibility disputes.

Types of E-Rate Appeals We Handle

FCC Appeals

Waiver Requests
