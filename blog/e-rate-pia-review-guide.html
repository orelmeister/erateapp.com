E-Rate PIA Review: How to Respond and Get Approved

PIA review is where E-Rate applications succeed or fail. Learn exactly what USAC reviewers look for, how to respond to common questions, and the strategies our experts use to achieve a 98% approval rate.

After you file your Form 471, your application enters the Program Integrity Assurance (PIA) review‚ÄîUSAC's quality control process. This is the single most important phase of your E-Rate application, and how you handle it determines whether you receive funding.

Our team has guided thousands of applicants through PIA review with a 98% approval rate. Here's everything you need to know.

What Is PIA Review?

PIA (Program Integrity Assurance) is USAC's internal review process where trained reviewers examine your E-Rate application for:

  • Compliance with all FCC rules and program requirements
  • Accuracy of information provided on forms
  • Eligibility of entities, services, and costs
  • Competitive bidding compliance per program rules
  • Cost-effectiveness of requested services

Not every application receives PIA questions. Some are approved without additional inquiry, particularly if you're a returning applicant with a clean track record. However, most first-time applicants and applications with certain flags will receive questions.

When Does PIA Review Happen?

PIA review typically begins in April after the Form 471 filing window closes and can continue through September or later. The timeline depends on:

  • Your discount level (higher-discount applicants are often reviewed first)
  • USAC's reviewer workload for that funding year
  • The complexity of your application
  • Whether your application was flagged for specific issues

The 8 Most Common PIA Questions (And How to Answer Them)

Question 1

"Please provide your competitive bidding documentation."

This is the most common PIA request. USAC wants to verify that you followed proper competitive bidding procedures.

Best Response: Provide your complete Form 470, all bids received, your bid evaluation matrix with scoring criteria, board approval documentation, and the signed contract. Include a narrative explaining your evaluation process and why you selected the winning vendor.
Question 2

"How was price weighted as the primary factor in vendor selection?"

FCC rules require that price be the most heavily weighted factor in your bid evaluation.

Best Response: Show your scoring rubric with specific point values. Price should be worth at least 30-40% of total points. Explain how you calculated price scores and demonstrate that the lowest-priced bid either won or that a higher-priced vendor won based on clearly documented non-price factors.
Question 3

"Please provide a copy of your signed contract or service agreement."

USAC verifies that contract terms match Form 471 details.

Best Response: Submit the fully executed contract showing: both parties' signatures, start and end dates, service descriptions, pricing, and any E-Rate-specific terms. Ensure the contract dates are after your Form 470 and 28-day waiting period.
Question 4

"Please verify the eligibility of [entity name]."

Common for charter schools, new applicants, or entities with data discrepancies.

Best Response: Provide your NCES school code, state education agency documentation confirming your school's status, and documentation showing your nonprofit status. For libraries, include your state library agency's listing.
Question 5

"The services requested may include ineligible components. Please clarify."

USAC flags services that might not be on the eligible services list.

Best Response: Break down your service/equipment list and map each item to the specific ESL category. If any items serve dual purposes (eligible and ineligible), provide a cost allocation showing only the eligible portion is being requested.
Question 6

"Please confirm your NSLP data and discount calculation."

USAC verifies that your discount percentage is accurate.

Best Response: Provide your most recent NSLP eligibility report from your nutrition department or state education agency. If using CEP, provide your ISP verification letter. Include student enrollment counts that match your EPC profile.
Question 7

"Please explain the relationship between FRN costs and the contract pricing."

USAC checks that Form 471 amounts match contracted prices.

Best Response: Create a clear cost reconciliation showing how each FRN amount derives from your contract. Include unit prices, quantities, and totals. If there are differences (e.g., you're only requesting partial funding), explain why.
Question 8

"Was the vendor selected in the competitive bidding for this FRN an existing vendor?"

USAC investigates potential conflicts of interest or pre-selection.

Best Response: Be honest. If yes, explain that the Form 470 was open to all vendors, the evaluation was fair, and the existing vendor won based on documented criteria. Emphasize that you did not restrict competition or give the incumbent an unfair advantage.

PIA Response Do's and Don'ts

DO:

  • Respond within 5 business days (even though you have 15)
  • Answer every question directly
  • Attach supporting documentation
  • Be transparent about errors
  • Check your EPC portal daily
  • Include a cover letter summarizing your responses

DON'T:

  • Wait until the deadline to respond
  • Give vague or incomplete answers
  • Argue with or criticize the reviewer
  • Submit documents without context
  • Assume "no news is good news"
  • Ignore questions you can't fully answer

You have 15 business days to respond to PIA questions. Missing this deadline is an automatic denial. If you need more time, contact the reviewer immediately and request an extension‚Äîthey can sometimes grant additional time for complex requests.

PIA Review Preparation Checklist

Prepare these documents before PIA review begins so you can respond quickly:

  • Form 470 filing confirmation with posting date
  • All bids received (including losing bids)
  • Bid evaluation matrix with scoring criteria and results
  • Board meeting minutes approving the vendor selection
  • Signed contract with matching terms and dates
  • NSLP eligibility documentation or CEP verification
  • CIPA compliance documentation (internet safety policy)
  • Technology plan or needs assessment
  • Cost allocation documentation (if services are shared)
  • Entity eligibility documentation (NCES code, nonprofit status)

What If PIA Review Goes Wrong?

If your application is denied after PIA review, you still have options:
