E-Rate Eligible Services List 2026: What's Covered

Not sure what E-Rate will pay for? This comprehensive guide breaks down every eligible service and product for FY2026, organized by Category 1 and Category 2.

The E-Rate program provides discounts on two categories of services for eligible schools and libraries. Understanding exactly what's covered‚Äîand what's not‚Äîis essential for filing an accurate Form 471 and maximizing your funding.

USAC publishes an updated Eligible Services List (ESL) each funding year. Here's what you need to know for FY2026.

Category 1

Data Transmission Services & Internet Access

Category 1 covers the connectivity that gets internet to your building and between your buildings. Discounts range from 20% to 90% based on your poverty percentage.

Category 2

Internal Connections (Wi-Fi & Network Equipment)

Category 2 covers the network equipment inside your buildings that distributes internet to users. Each school gets a $167/student budget over a 5-year cycle. Learn more in our Category 2 funding guide.

What's NOT Eligible for E-Rate

These are commonly requested items that are not eligible for E-Rate funding:

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Ineligible Items

  • End-user devices: Laptops, tablets, Chromebooks, desktops
  • Servers: Application servers, file servers, email servers
  • Software: Operating systems, productivity suites, learning management systems
  • Voice/telephone services: Removed from E-Rate as of FY2020
  • Video surveillance: Security cameras and DVR systems
  • Printers and peripherals: All end-user peripherals
  • Classroom AV equipment: Projectors, interactive displays, speakers
  • Professional development: Teacher training, PD workshops

Key Rules to Remember

The "2-in-5" Rule for Category 2

Applicants can request Category 2 funding in any 2 out of 5 funding years within their budget cycle. You don't have to use all your budget at once‚Äîstrategic planning helps maximize your investment.

Cost Allocation for Shared Equipment

If equipment serves both eligible and ineligible purposes (e.g., a firewall that also manages VoIP), you must allocate costs appropriately. Only the portion supporting eligible services can be funded.

Manufacturer Discontinuation

USAC does not require specific manufacturers or brands. You can choose any manufacturer as long as the product type is on the eligible services list.
