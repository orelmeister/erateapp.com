What Happens After E-Rate Form 471 Is Filed?

You've submitted your Form 471‚Äîcongratulations! But the E-Rate process is far from over. Here's exactly what to expect in the months ahead, from PIA review to receiving your funding.

Filing your Form 471 is a major milestone in the E-Rate process, but it's just the beginning of the post-application phase. Understanding what happens next helps you prepare for PIA review, avoid costly mistakes, and ultimately get your funding approved.

The Post-Form 471 Timeline

Here's the typical timeline after submitting your Form 471:

March ‚Äì April

Form 471 Filing Window Closes

All Form 471 applications are submitted. USAC begins processing applications in the order received, starting with the highest-discount applicants. Check current deadlines for exact dates.

April ‚Äì September

PIA Review Period

USAC's Program Integrity Assurance (PIA) team reviews your application. This is the most critical phase‚Äîyou'll likely receive questions that require prompt, thorough responses. Learn more in our PIA review guide.

June ‚Äì December

Funding Commitment Decision Letters (FCDLs)

USAC issues FCDLs in waves throughout the year. Your FCDL confirms your approved funding amounts. Higher-discount applicants typically receive decisions first.

July 1 ‚Äì June 30

Service Delivery Period

Services must be delivered within the funding year (July 1 ‚Äì June 30). For Category 2 equipment, installation should be completed and equipment operational by the end of the funding year.

October 28 (following year)

Invoice Deadline

You have 120 days after the service delivery deadline to submit your invoices (Form 472 BEAR or Form 474 SPI). Missing this deadline means forfeiting your approved funding.

Phase 1: PIA Review‚ÄîThe Make-or-Break Stage

PIA review is where many applications succeed or fail. USAC reviewers examine your application for compliance with all program rules, including:

  • Competitive bidding compliance: Did you follow all bidding rules?
  • Form accuracy: Do the details on your Form 471 match your Form 470 and contract?
  • Entity eligibility: Are all listed schools/libraries eligible for E-Rate?
  • Service eligibility: Are all requested services on the eligible services list?
  • Cost effectiveness: Are prices reasonable for the services requested?

What to Expect from PIA

USAC will send questions through the EPC portal. Common PIA requests include:

  1. Copies of your competitive bidding documentation
  2. Bid evaluation matrices or scoring sheets
  3. Signed contracts or service agreements
  4. Clarification on service descriptions or costs
  5. Proof of entity eligibility (for new applicants)
  6. Technology plan or needs assessment documentation

‚ö†Ô∏è 15-Day Response Deadline

You typically have 15 business days to respond to PIA questions. If you miss this deadline, your application will be denied. Check your EPC portal and registered email daily during review season.

How to Ace PIA Review

  • Respond quickly: Don't wait until the deadline. Early responses show good faith.
  • Be thorough: Answer every question completely. Vague responses lead to follow-up questions.
  • Provide documentation: Attach supporting documents even if not specifically requested.
  • Be honest: If you made a mistake, acknowledge it and explain how it was corrected.

Our PIA review support service provides expert guidance throughout this critical phase, ensuring your responses are complete, timely, and compliant.

Phase 2: Receiving Your FCDL

The Funding Commitment Decision Letter (FCDL) is USAC's official response to your application. It can contain three types of decisions:

  • Funded: Your request is approved at the amounts listed
  • Denied: Your request is rejected, with reasons provided
  • Partially funded: Some FRNs are approved while others are denied, or amounts are reduced

If You're Approved

Review your FCDL carefully to confirm the approved amounts match your expectations. Then proceed with service delivery and prepare for invoicing.

If You're Denied

Don't give up. You have 60 days from the FCDL date to file an appeal. Many denials are overturned, especially for procedural issues. Read our guide on how to win an E-Rate appeal or contact our appeals team immediately.

Phase 3: Service Delivery and Invoicing

Once you have your funding commitment, the focus shifts to receiving services and getting paid.

Service Delivery

  • Category 1 services (internet) run for the full funding year
  • Category 2 equipment must be installed and operational by the end of the funding year
  • Keep records of delivery dates, installation completion, and equipment locations

Invoicing Options

You have two ways to receive your E-Rate discount:

  • Form 472 (BEAR): You pay the vendor in full, then request reimbursement from USAC for the discount amount
  • Form 474 (SPI): The vendor bills USAC directly for the discount portion, and you pay only your share

üí° Pro Tip

Most schools prefer the SPI method because it reduces upfront costs. However, the BEAR method gives you more control over when payments are made. Discuss with your vendor which option works best.

Document Retention Requirements

USAC requires you to retain all E-Rate documentation for at least 10 years from the last date of service. This includes:

  • Forms 470, 471, and all supporting documentation
  • All bids received and bid evaluation records
  • Signed contracts and amendments
  • Invoices and proof of payment
  • Correspondence with USAC and vendors
  • Technology plans and needs assessments
